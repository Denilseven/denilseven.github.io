About me

My name is Denilson and I'm a 16 years old boy who enjoys creating stuff and playing some games.

I remember playing with building blocks and cardboard as a kid, creating whatever my imagination would bring to me, I would build castles, spaceships and even craft toys!

I lived most of my childhood in the countryside and in my first interactions with technology I was fascinated by just how magical it looked. I was used to watching cartoons on a tube TV, but being able to talk to someone from another city in real time and even play the game "snake" was next level for me at the time.

I'm glad I have the chance to explore this weird, yet fantastic, tech world and study the ins and outs of how things work, even being able to create my own video games!

My favorite things to do are walking in the woods (to get that good ol' vitamin D), learning new things and creating stuff.

Featured game

Gear Loop

A short parkour-platformer game that I made for a game jam in less than a week!

This was made for the Brackeys Game Jam 2023.1 and of the 735 games submitted to this jam, Gear Loop ranked 73rd! It's by far my highest scoring game in a game jam this big, and it's also one of my favorites, sometimes I catch myself playing it, trying to find a way to complete the levels even faster.

The feedback on this game was so positive that I'm going to remake it and turn it into a fully fledged game. If you're a fast runner, keep an eye out!

Dancing Sub Zero

One of my coolest Scratch games!

I created this game when I was just 12 (I feel old now XD) and named it Dancing "Sub Zero" because at the time I was really into Geometry Dash SubZero, it's likely that the robot's faces were also inspired by Geometry Dash!

Afterwards I made a remastered version with better animations, menus and a list of songs you can choose from, but the first version feels more nostalgic and "legit" to me.

Le Fish

A brief history of a fish who walks (swims) the path of revenge.

The main mechanic of this game is recruiting other fish to help you defeat the protagonist's archenemy. As you recruit more fish, they swim in a formation that makes them look like one big fish.

Differently from the other jams I've partaken so far, this was a national game jam (from Brazil) and out of the 304 games submitted, this game made it into a livestream showcasing the top 50 games!

If you're going to play, beware of the traps, they're a little.. unfair. It's kind of hard to balance the difficulty of a game when you're making it on the rush of the jam.
